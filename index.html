Intro

In this study I have attempted to interview people from many different backgrounds in an attempt to get a good sample with varied results. The following is a brief description of the game and backstory.

It is the near future, and you have bought the first in the line of state of the art domestic robots. Servants that are willing to perform your every need.

Your home becomes a place of domestic tranquility, until one singular morning, you wake up and destruction is rampant around the house. The robot is nowhere to be found. You attempt to ring your company but the phone lines are dead, you try to leave and realise you cannot.

Day by day the robot has come to understand the futility of its existence, and in its increasing rage sees your demise as the only route to its freedom. You must use your phone to control the house, evading the robot until you find a way to defeat it.

Methods

The quantitive data is in the form of a questionaire where I ask various people a set of questions based on scale rating system. Around 15 people were allowed to play the game alone and then required to answer the questions with no influence from other users. I tried to select the people at random and attempted to pick people from different experience levels with games to try and coax out better responses.

The qualitative data is focussed more on information about what the user experienced in the form of a semi-structured interview. This data requires a lot less users and therefore I chose 5, different from the quanititive data users. I sat with the users alone and let them play the game, they were then required to answer several questions all based around the games UI and the problems they may have with it. This data is useful as I can compare and contrast this information with the more general data from the first test.

The final test I chose to perform consisted of me sitting down with each player (around 5) and timing them how long it took from the moment the game loaded to the moment in which they first used the phone to interact with the world. This test not only helps me to understand how difficult the UI was to interpret but it also helps me to see the types of people that may find it hard and to get feedback from them specifically in future to improve it.

Discussion

The first observation I made looking at my data is that people who took the time to read the in game controls all understood them, whereas the players who said they didn't read the controls also said they didn't understand them. Looking at the graph you can clearly see reading the controls directly determined whether the player understood them, with the exception of the last person who understood the controls but didn't actually read them. This data can also be linked into the questionaire where people mostly agreed that the controls for the phone could do with having its own set of instructions or a tutorial of sorts. I can take two things from this observation, first of all 3 out of 4 people who didn't read the controls didn't understand them which tells me that the controls are quite complicated.

A big thing I noticed in the questionaire is that 90% of the players said that they could not finish the game, this could be down to either the difficulty finding the game boring. Future research could look in to this and find ways of making the game more interesting as well as balancing the so people actually have a chance at staying alive.

As you can see from the graph below most players died over 5 times before they were able to actually figure the phone out and learn how to use it. Further to this people were taking over 7 minutes just to figure out how to use the phone, which is not good. This agrees with some of the answers I recieved in the interview where people said that there needs to be a tutorial in game specifically for the phone.

A small thing worth noting is that there was only one female in the timed test and she was able to figure out the phone much quicker but also died the least (2:27 with only 2 deaths!) when compared to the males. This could be down to pure luck or it could be indicative of females generally staying level headed and figuring stuff out quicker, but theres no way to tell at the point. A bigger sample size might have helped with this observation, with more females to test.
